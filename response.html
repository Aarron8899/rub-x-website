Open Letter to the International Community

This is the full text of the public appeal issued by the Rub-X project, calling attention to systemic barriers and neglected innovation in the global rubber industry.

To international institutions, responsible governments, and fair media:

I am the inventor of the Rub-X system‚Äîan ultra-low-cost, energy-flexible, terrain-adaptable automation breakthrough for rubber tapping, designed to protect both forests and laborers.

Since 2022, I have reached out to dozens of agencies across countries, including the UN system, WIPO, FAO, major research bodies, and industry councils.

While the technology has been completed, verified, and published, the response remains silence or evasion‚Äîmostly citing "field deployment" as a barrier while refusing to host or support such deployment.

This reveals a troubling pattern: Instead of enabling innovation, the global system imposes a burden of proof that independent inventors cannot meet alone, even when their solution is the only one of its kind.

This is not just about one project‚Äîit is a systemic failure. I ask that the Rub-X case be recognized as an example of green technology structurally marginalized, and that institutions, especially those in the U.S., help restore fairness to the process.

The world cannot afford to reject simple, tested, scalable solutions just because they come from the outside.
